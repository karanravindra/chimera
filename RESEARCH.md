## Sweep 1 -- 2026-07-01 -- DC-AE pipeline research

### Adversarial

#### Taming Transformers for High-Resolution Image Synthesis (VQGAN) (2021, CVPR 2021 (Oral))
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2012.09841
- **What:** Introduces the convolutional VQGAN autoencoder/tokenizer that couples a pixel + LPIPS perceptual reconstruction loss with a patch-based (PatchGAN) discriminator to make an AE compress hard while keeping high perceptual fidelity, plus an automatic adaptive weight for the adversarial term so the practitioner never hand-tunes the GAN-vs-reconstruction balance. This is the origin of the loss recipe that essentially every modern AE/VAE/tokenizer (SD-VAE, DC-AE, TiTok, LlamaGen, ViTok) still uses.
- **Why:** The single most directly applicable paper for the task: it is the reference implementation of "add a GAN term to sharpen AE reconstructions," and it solves the two hardest practical problems on a short budget on small data -- how to weight the GAN term so it doesn't blow up the MSE+LPIPS objective, and when to turn the discriminator on. The adaptive-lambda trick is the most widely copied loss-weighting mechanism in tokenizer/VAE training; getting these knobs right is usually the difference between rFID improving vs. the run diverging.
- **How:** Total loss = L_rec (L1 or L2) + L_perceptual (LPIPS) + lambda * L_GAN, with a hinge (or non-saturating) adversarial loss and a PatchGAN discriminator (a shallow N-layer conv net outputting a per-patch real/fake map). Two load-bearing tricks: (1) the ADAPTIVE GAN WEIGHT lambda = ||grad_{last_dec_layer}(L_rec)|| / (||grad_{last_dec_layer}(L_GAN)|| + eps), computed via autograd w.r.t. the decoder's LAST conv layer only (cheap), detached, clamped to [0, 1e4], and multiplied by a fixed disc_weight -- so the GAN gradient is auto-normalized to the reconstruction gradient each step; (2) a DISCRIMINATOR WARMUP -- L_GAN is gated to zero until a fixed step count (disc_start). Reference impl: taming-transformers vqperceptual.py `calculate_adaptive_weight`, ~10 lines.
- **Relevance:** Drop-in for the DC-AE-on-AFHQ trainer: adaptive lambda on the decoder last layer + hinge + PatchGAN + disc warmup is the highest-leverage loss-weighting change to cut rFID without destabilizing short AFHQ 128px runs.

#### Which Training Methods for GANs do actually Converge? (2018, ICML 2018)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1801.04406
- **What:** The theoretical paper that introduces and analyzes the R1 (and symmetric R2) zero-centered gradient penalties, proving that vanilla GAN training need not converge but that penalizing the discriminator's gradient on real data restores local convergence.
- **Why:** On a small dataset like AFHQ and a short window, discriminator instability/overfitting is the main failure mode that makes the GAN term hurt rather than help rFID. This paper explains why a zero-centered gradient penalty (rather than WGAN-GP's one-centered penalty) is the right regularizer, and its manifold argument (real/fake distributions on lower-dimensional manifolds) is precisely the regime an AE reconstruction GAN operates in.
- **How:** Add R1 = (gamma/2) * E_{x~real}[ ||grad_x D(x)||^2 ] to the discriminator loss (penalize the squared gradient norm of D w.r.t. its real inputs). R2 is the mirror term on generated/reconstructed samples. Computed with torch.autograd.grad(D(x).sum(), x, create_graph=True) then .pow(2).sum over spatial dims. gamma is the key hyperparameter; larger gamma = more smoothing/stability at some cost to sharpness.
- **Relevance:** Justifies and specifies the R1/R2 penalty to add to the PatchGAN/StyleGAN discriminator to keep it from overfitting AFHQ in short runs -- the theory behind the stabilizer.

#### Analyzing and Improving the Image Quality of StyleGAN (StyleGAN2) (2020, CVPR 2020)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1912.04958
- **What:** Introduces the StyleGAN2 architecture and, most relevant here, the practical training recipe that made R1 regularization standard: R1 gradient penalty applied with "lazy regularization" (only every k steps) on a residual-downsampling convolutional discriminator.
- **Why:** It defines the main alternative to PatchGAN -- a full-image StyleGAN-style discriminator (residual conv blocks + minibatch-stddev) with a single R1 penalty -- and shows the efficiency tricks (lazy reg) that make R1 cheap enough for a consumer-GPU budget. Useful when a per-patch discriminator's local receptive field isn't catching global structure errors in reconstructions.
- **How:** Discriminator: strided/residual conv blocks downsampling to a scalar logit, plus a minibatch-standard-deviation layer near the end to discourage mode collapse. Regularization: R1 zero-centered penalty applied LAZILY (e.g. once every 16 D steps, with gamma scaled by the interval) so the extra double-backward cost is amortized. Non-saturating logistic (softplus) adversarial loss. Path-length reg is generator-side and not needed for an AE reconstruction setting.
- **Relevance:** Gives the StyleGAN-discriminator-vs-PatchGAN alternative plus the lazy-R1 implementation pattern that keeps gradient-penalty overhead affordable on a single consumer GPU.

#### The GAN is dead; long live the GAN! A Modern GAN Baseline (R3GAN) (2024, NeurIPS 2024)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.05441
- **What:** A 2024/25 paper that strips GANs down to a minimal, well-behaved baseline (R3GAN) by combining a relativistic pairing GAN loss with BOTH R1 and R2 zero-centered gradient penalties, proving local convergence, and showing it beats StyleGAN2 on FFHQ/ImageNet/CIFAR/StackedMNIST with no ad-hoc tricks.
- **Why:** The current best-practice, minimal-hyperparameter answer to "which adversarial loss + which gradient penalty should I use in 2025." Its central empirical finding -- that R1 ALONE causes gradient explosion on fake samples and you need R2 as well to stabilize both distributions -- is exactly the subtle failure that bites short, small-data AE-GAN training, and the fix is a one-line addition.
- **How:** Use a relativistic (RpGAN) loss: D scores the difference between a real and a fake sample rather than each independently, mitigating mode dropping. Add zero-centered penalties on BOTH real (R1) and fake/reconstructed (R2) samples: L_D = RpGAN + (gamma/2)(R1 + R2). Pair with a modern ResNet-style discriminator (no minibatch-stddev, no progressive growing, no equalized-lr, no style-mixing). The ablation shows R1+R2 together is what unlocks stable training of the plain loss.
- **Relevance:** A modern, minimal-tuning drop-in: relativistic loss + R1+R2 on real and reconstructed images, the most robust discriminator-side setup for stable rFID gains in short AFHQ runs.

### Small-data GAN

#### Training Generative Adversarial Networks with Limited Data (StyleGAN2-ADA) (2020, NeurIPS)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.06676
- **What:** Introduces Adaptive Discriminator Augmentation (ADA): a wide pipeline of invertible image augmentations (geometric, color, filtering, additive noise, cutout) applied to both real and fake images fed to the discriminator, with the augmentation probability p tuned automatically during training. The canonical solution for GAN training on few-thousand-image datasets (validated directly on AFHQ and small FFHQ subsets).
- **Why:** Directly targets our exact regime: AFHQ (~5k images/class) with short training windows, where the adversarial discriminator overfits fast and the GAN loss term destabilizes reconstruction. ADA is the single most battle-tested stabilizer for this dataset and requires no dataset-size hyperparameter guessing.
- **How:** Apply a fixed set of differentiable, invertible augmentations to every image D sees (both real x and decoder output x_hat), each with probability p. Track a discriminator overfitting heuristic r_t = E[sign(D_logits_real)] over a rolling window; if r_t exceeds a target (~0.6), nudge p up by a fixed step every N=4 minibatches, else nudge it down. Because augmentations are invertible/probabilistic, D cannot fully exploit them and the decoder never sees leaked augmentation artifacts (only if p->1). Only D's inputs are augmented; the AE's MSE+LPIPS terms on clean images are untouched.
- **Relevance:** Drop-in stabilizer for the patch-discriminator in a DC-AE decoder-refinement stage on AFHQ 128px; adaptive p removes the need to tune augmentation strength for a short run.

#### Differentiable Augmentation for Data-Efficient GAN Training (DiffAugment) (2020, NeurIPS)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.10738
- **What:** Proposes DiffAugment: apply the SAME differentiable augmentation (translation, cutout, color) to both real and fake images, and crucially back-propagate through the augmentation into the generator, not just the discriminator. Matches StyleGAN2 FID with 20% of data and works from 100-image datasets up.
- **Why:** A minimal, ~30-line technique that regularizes D without shifting the target distribution, and the requirement that gradients flow through the augmentation into the generator is a subtle correctness point most naive "augment D's inputs" implementations get wrong. It is lighter than ADA (no adaptive controller) so it is a good first stabilizer to try under a tight compute budget.
- **How:** Wrap D's forward passes: D(T(x)) for real and D(T(G(z))) for fake, using the identical random transform T within a minibatch. Use only differentiable ops (grid-sample translation, differentiable cutout via masking, additive/multiplicative color transforms) so d/dG flows through T. In the AE setting, T is applied to both the ground-truth image and the decoder reconstruction before D scores them; the reconstruction (MSE/LPIPS) losses stay on un-augmented pixels. Color+Translation+Cutout is the default strong policy for the low-data regime.
- **Relevance:** Cheapest way to keep the AE's adversarial discriminator from memorizing AFHQ in a short run; the differentiable-through-augmentation detail is the load-bearing implementation constraint for the decoder gradient.

#### Regularizing Generative Adversarial Networks under Limited Data (LeCam regularization) (2021, CVPR)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.03310
- **What:** Adds a lightweight regularization term to the discriminator loss that anchors D's outputs on real and fake samples to exponential moving averages of their past values, shown to correspond to minimizing the LeCam f-divergence, provably more robust than the standard JS divergence under limited data. Explicitly designed to complement (not replace) DiffAugment/ADA.
- **Why:** An orthogonal, near-free add-on to augmentation-based methods: augmentation limits D's input overfitting while LeCam constrains D's output dynamics, and stacking the two gives the best small-data FID in the paper. Two extra EMA scalars and one extra loss term make it trivial to bolt onto an existing AE+GAN trainer.
- **How:** Maintain two EMAs: alpha_real (EMA of D(real) logits) and alpha_fake (EMA of D(fake) logits), updated each step with decay ~0.99. Add R_LC = ||D(real) - alpha_fake||^2 + ||D(fake) - alpha_real||^2 to the discriminator objective, weighted by lambda (~0.001-0.1). This penalizes D for pushing real and fake scores apart faster than the running averages, damping the runaway D that causes GAN-loss instability. Generator/decoder loss is unchanged; only D's update gains the regularizer.
- **Relevance:** Stacks on top of DiffAugment/ADA in the AE decoder-refinement stage to further stabilize the adversarial term with two EMA scalars and one extra D loss, protecting the MSE/LPIPS objective from D blow-ups.

### Progressive / latent adaptation

#### Deep Compression Autoencoder for Efficient High-Resolution Diffusion Models (DC-AE) (2024 (ICLR 2025), ICLR 2025)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.10733
- **What:** The namesake of the target architecture. Introduces DC-AE, a high-spatial-compression (32x-128x) autoencoder family, built on two ideas: Residual Autoencoding and, most relevant here, Decoupled High-Resolution Adaptation -- a three-phase training recipe that recovers reconstruction fidelity cheaply.
- **Why:** The primary, on-lane source. It shows you do NOT need to train the whole AE end-to-end at high res to get good rFID: a low-res pretrain plus a tiny selective-layer adaptation phase closes most of the generalization gap. It reports the exact failure mode we care about (f64 rFID degrading 0.50 -> 7.40 when moving 256->1024px) and fixes it with a small fraction of the compute/memory (153.98GB -> 67.81GB), mapping directly onto a consumer-GPU, short-training-window budget.
- **How:** Three decoupled phases. Phase 1: full-model training at low resolution (256px) with L1 + LPIPS + PatchGAN. Phase 2 (high-res latent adaptation): train at high res but freeze almost everything and fine-tune only the MIDDLE layers (encoder head / decoder input layers that define the latent) with reconstruction loss ONLY -- GAN is removed here because "reconstruction loss alone is sufficient for content/semantics, GAN mainly improves local details," so decoupling stops GAN from corrupting the latent. Phase 3 (low-res local refinement): return to 256px, freeze encoder + middle decoder, fine-tune only the decoder HEAD (output layers) with PatchGAN to restore local texture without destabilizing the latent. Key lever: partition the net into encoder-head / middle / decoder-head groups and gate requires_grad + loss terms per phase.
- **Relevance:** Directly defines our model and gives a compute-cheap recipe: pretrain the DC-AE at low res, then fine-tune only latent/middle layers (no GAN) and finally only the decoder head (with GAN) to lift AFHQ 128px rFID within a short window.

#### DC-AE 1.5: Accelerating Diffusion Model Convergence with Structured Latent Space (2025, arXiv preprint (Aug 2025); MIT Han Lab / DC-AI)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.00413
- **What:** Direct follow-up to DC-AE from the same group. Adds a Structured Latent Space (front latent channels carry object structure, later channels carry fine detail) plus an Augmented Diffusion Training objective, targeting the fact that raising latent channel count improves reconstruction but slows/worsens downstream generation.
- **Why:** It refines the DC-AE latent-adaptation story with the reconstruction-vs-generation tradeoff that matters when you push channels up to chase rFID. Even if we only care about reconstruction, its channel-ordering / structured-latent training is a low-cost regularizer on the same latent-adaptation phase, and it demonstrates the same modular multi-phase, latent-targeted training philosophy.
- **How:** Impose a training-time structure so front latent channels capture coarse object structure and trailing channels the residual detail (a nested/ordered-dropout-style objective on latent channels), and add auxiliary diffusion objectives on the "object" channels. In our reconstruction-only setting the transferable piece is the structured-latent regularization applied during the latent-adaptation phase, letting you add channels for rFID without the usual convergence penalty.
- **Relevance:** If we increase DC-AE latent channels to push AFHQ rFID down, this gives the channel-structuring trick to add during latent adaptation so extra capacity actually helps rather than destabilizing training.

#### Progressive Growing of GANs for Improved Quality, Stability, and Variation (2017 (ICLR 2018), ICLR 2018)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1710.10196
- **What:** The foundational progressive-resolution training paper. Trains generator+discriminator starting at low resolution and incrementally adds layers to reach higher resolutions, with smooth (alpha-faded) layer insertion.
- **Why:** The origin of the "low-res first, grow to high-res" curriculum that DC-AE's decoupled adaptation is a modern, freeze-based specialization of. On a consumer GPU with short windows it is the canonical evidence that low-res pretraining both speeds up and stabilizes training (most epochs spent cheaply at small resolution). The fade-in mechanism is a concrete, still-relevant recipe for adding a resolution stage to an AE without shocking the optimization.
- **How:** Maintain a resolution schedule (4x4 -> ... -> target). When adding a new higher-res block, linearly fade it in via a residual skip weighted by alpha in [0,1] over many iterations so the new layers activate gradually; discriminator mirrors this with a downsample path. For AFHQ 128px: train encoder/decoder at 32/64px, then fade in the 128px blocks rather than retraining cold -- pairs naturally with the DC-AE freeze-then-adapt phases.
- **Relevance:** Provides the progressive-resolution curriculum and smooth fade-in mechanism to reach AFHQ 128px cheaply and stably, complementing DC-AE's freeze-based high-res adaptation.

#### EQ-VAE: Equivariance Regularized Latent Space for Improved Generative Image Modeling (2025, arXiv preprint (valeo.ai), Feb 2025)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.09509
- **What:** A simple equivariance regularizer added to autoencoder training so that spatial transformations (scaling, rotation) applied to the image correspond to the same transformation applied to the latent, producing a smoother, lower-complexity latent WITHOUT degrading reconstruction.
- **Why:** A nearly free add-on loss that improves latent structure (and downstream generation, e.g. 7x DiT-XL speedup) while keeping reconstruction fidelity flat -- works on both continuous and discrete AEs, applied as a short fine-tune (5 epochs on SD-VAE). Low-risk to bolt onto our existing DC-AE.
- **How:** For a transform T (scaling/rotation), enforce equivariance by penalizing the mismatch between encoding the transformed image and transforming the latent: regularize dec(T(enc(x))) toward T(x) (and/or match enc(T(x)) to T(enc(x))) in addition to the standard reconstruction + LPIPS objective. Implemented as a fine-tuning add-on; 5 epochs suffices, reconstruction quality preserved.
- **Relevance:** A cheap regularization fine-tune we can layer on our AFHQ DC-AE to clean up the latent without hurting rFID, useful when we later attach a generator; secondary to the pure-rFID levers above.

### EMA & optimizer

#### Learnings from Scaling Visual Tokenizers for Reconstruction and Generation (ViTok) (2025, arXiv:2501.09755 (Meta FAIR), Jan 2025)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.09755
- **What:** A systematic study of ViT-based continuous tokenizers (Llama-style, RoPE + SwiGLU) that scales each component to derive the scaling laws governing reconstruction rFID, and publishes a clean, reproducible two-stage training recipe -- optimizer, LR schedule, EMA policy, and per-term loss weights -- that is a strong default for AE/tokenizer rFID.
- **Why:** It answers the exact operational questions in this subtopic with concrete, recent, battle-tested values (when to turn EMA on, AdamW betas, LR schedule, how to stage the GAN term), AND isolates the single most actionable architectural lever: reconstruction is governed almost entirely by E = L*c, the total number of floating-point values in the latent (log E vs log rFID is strongly linear), NOT by encoder size or FLOPs. The encoder can be tiny while the decoder is where you spend capacity.
- **How:** Asymmetric design: small encoder (~43M) + larger decoder is optimal -- scaling the encoder is neutral-to-harmful, scaling the decoder drops rFID (1.6->1.3 at E=4096), but doubling E dominates (rFID->0.8). Stage 1 (reconstruction): AdamW (beta1=0.9, beta2=0.95, wd=1e-4), peak LR 1e-4 per 256 batch, cosine decay, NO EMA; loss = MSE + 1e-3*KL + 1.0*LPIPS, no GAN. Stage 2 (GAN fine-tune): FREEZE the encoder, fine-tune only the decoder, add GAN lambda=1.0 (keeping MSE+1e-3*KL+1.0*LPIPS), discriminator LR 2e-5 with 25k-step LINEAR WARMUP, and turn ON weight EMA at decay 0.9999. Each stage ~100k steps. Key heuristics: EMA (0.9999) matters most during the volatile GAN phase, not the smooth L1/LPIPS phase; scale LR linearly with batch; warm up the discriminator to avoid early collapse. Best 256p ImageNet rFID 0.50 with 2-5x fewer FLOPs than conv baselines.
- **Relevance:** A ready-to-adapt recipe skeleton for short AFHQ 128px runs: cosine+AdamW(0.9,0.95), raise latent channel count/E, make the decoder bigger than the encoder, no EMA in the reconstruction phase then EMA 0.9999 + a discriminator LR-warmup once the GAN term is enabled.

#### GradNorm: Gradient Normalization for Adaptive Loss Balancing in Deep Multitask Networks (2018, ICML 2018)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.02257
- **What:** A general algorithm that learns per-loss weights so that every loss term produces gradients of comparable magnitude at a shared layer, balanced by each term's relative training speed. Treats MSE / LPIPS / (KL) / GAN terms as "tasks".
- **Why:** Autoencoder rFID training is a genuine multi-objective problem where terms have wildly different scales and converge at different rates; fixed hand-tuned weights are fragile in short windows. GradNorm is the principled, gradient-normalized alternative that generalizes VQGAN's single-term adaptive-lambda to all terms at once.
- **How:** Pick a shared layer W (last shared decoder layer). Each step compute G_i = ||grad_W(w_i * L_i)|| and the average G_bar. Track relative inverse training rate r_i = (L_i/L_i^0) / mean_j(L_j/L_j^0). Target = G_bar * r_i^alpha (detached). Update the weights w_i by minimizing L_grad = sum_i |G_i - target_i| via a small separate optimizer step on the w_i, then renormalize sum_i w_i = #terms. alpha (~0.5-1.5) controls how aggressively slow terms get boosted.
- **Relevance:** If VQGAN's single adaptive lambda isn't enough, GradNorm auto-balances MSE vs LPIPS vs GAN at one shared decoder layer with just one extra hyperparameter (alpha) -- well suited to consumer-GPU tuning budgets.

#### Multi-Task Learning Using Uncertainty to Weigh Losses for Scene Geometry and Semantics (2018, CVPR 2018)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1705.07115
- **What:** Learns loss weights as free parameters via homoscedastic (task) uncertainty: each loss is divided by a learned noise variance and a log-variance regularizer prevents the trivial solution. Cheaper and more stable than GradNorm (no per-step gradient-norm computation).
- **Why:** Gives an almost-free automatic loss-weighting scheme for the MSE/LPIPS/KL mix that adds only one scalar per term and no extra backward passes -- ideal when compute is tight and you want to eliminate weight-sweep runs entirely. Complements the VQGAN lambda (which handles the GAN term) for the non-adversarial terms.
- **How:** For each loss L_i introduce a learnable log-variance s_i = log(sigma_i^2) as a model parameter. Total loss = sum_i [ exp(-s_i) * L_i + 0.5 * s_i ] (the 0.5*s_i regularizer stops sigma exploding). The s_i are trained by the same optimizer as the network; effective weight exp(-s_i) rises for well-fit, low-noise terms.
- **Relevance:** The lowest-overhead way to stop hand-tuning MSE-vs-LPIPS(-vs-KL) weights on AFHQ: add ~3 learnable scalars, no extra passes, and let the short training run learn the balance.

### Perceptual / frequency losses

#### Image Quality Assessment: Unifying Structure and Texture Similarity (DISTS) (2020 (arXiv); TPAMI 2022, IEEE TPAMI)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.07728
- **What:** A full-reference perceptual metric (DISTS) that unifies structure and texture similarity and is explicitly tolerant to texture resampling, correlating better with human judgment than LPIPS/SSIM/FSIM.
- **Why:** rFID on AFHQ is dominated by fur/whisker/texture fidelity, exactly where L2+LPIPS under-weight high-frequency texture. DISTS's texture term directly targets this and, unlike LPIPS, is invariant to imperceptible texture-patch resampling, so it rewards perceptually correct texture rather than pixel-exact copies, which should lower rFID.
- **How:** Pass reconstruction and target through a fixed ImageNet-pretrained VGG to get multi-scale feature maps at ~5 stages. Compute two SSIM-style terms per channel per scale: a STRUCTURE term on the correlation/variance of raw feature maps, and a TEXTURE term on the spatial MEANS of feature maps (global-avg-pooled per channel). Combine as a learned weighted sum (weights shipped in the released checkpoint). Fully differentiable; add as an additive loss term (loss = L1 + w_dists*DISTS(+ optional LPIPS)) via the official DISTS-pytorch package. Cheap: one frozen VGG forward pass, comparable to LPIPS.
- **Relevance:** Drop-in differentiable replacement/complement for LPIPS whose texture-statistics term is tailored to the high-frequency AFHQ detail that most affects rFID.

#### Focal Frequency Loss for Image Reconstruction and Synthesis (2021, ICCV 2021)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2012.12821
- **What:** A frequency-domain reconstruction loss that adaptively down-weights easy frequency bins and focuses learning on the hard-to-synthesize frequencies where real vs. generated images diverge.
- **Why:** Neural decoders have a well-documented spectral bias toward low frequencies (blurry recon), a primary rFID contributor. FFL directly closes the frequency-spectrum gap and is shown to help VAE reconstruction specifically, complementing spatial L1/LPIPS at negligible cost -- attractive under short training budgets.
- **How:** 2D FFT each image patch to complex spectrum; compute per-frequency squared distance between recon and target spectra; multiply each frequency's error by a dynamic "focal" weight = (normalized spectral distance)^alpha (alpha~1), stop-gradient on the weight so it just re-weights hard bins. Average over all frequencies -> scalar loss added to the objective (loss = L1 + LPIPS + w_ffl*FFL). Official focal-frequency-loss PyTorch module; one rfft per forward.
- **Relevance:** Directly attacks decoder spectral bias / high-frequency blur on AFHQ with a cheap, adaptive FFT loss ideal for short consumer-GPU runs.

#### A Loss Function for Generative Neural Networks Based on Watson's Perceptual Model (2020, NeurIPS 2020)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.15057
- **What:** A lightweight, differentiable perceptual loss built from Watson's human-vision model: a frequency-weighted (DCT/DFT) distance with luminance and contrast masking, extended to color and made shift-robust.
- **Why:** The closest published match to this task: it was designed and validated for VAE reconstruction, producing sharper, less-blurry samples than L2/SSIM while using far less compute than deep-feature (VGG/LPIPS) losses. Under short training windows on a consumer GPU, its low cost and no-pretrained-network requirement make it a strong LPIPS alternative.
- **How:** Block-DCT (or DFT for translation robustness) both images; take per-frequency coefficient differences; weight each frequency by a learnable perceptual sensitivity table initialized from Watson's model, and apply luminance + contrast-masking normalization (divide error by local coefficient energy). Sum weighted, masked coefficient distances -> scalar loss. Small differentiable module (author code released, torch); sensitivity weights can be fixed or fine-tuned. No external pretrained network needed, so memory/compute footprint is tiny.
- **Relevance:** A VAE-proven, frequency-weighted perceptual loss that is much cheaper than LPIPS/VGG and tailored to sharpen reconstructions in exactly the short-budget regime described.

#### Training Transformer Models by Wavelet Losses Improves Quantitative and Visual Performance in Single Image Super-Resolution (2024, CVPR Workshops 2024 (NTIRE 2024))
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.11273
- **What:** Shows that adding a wavelet-subband reconstruction loss (beyond pixel L1) recovers high-frequency detail and improves both PSNR and perceptual quality in image restoration models.
- **Why:** Wavelet losses give a spatially-localized frequency signal (unlike a global FFT), penalizing errors in high-frequency LH/HL/HH subbands where fur/edge detail lives -- a targeted way to reduce the high-frequency component of rFID without needing a discriminator or pretrained net.
- **How:** Apply a (stationary/discrete) 2D wavelet transform, e.g. Haar DWT, to recon and target, splitting each into LL (low) and LH/HL/HH (high-frequency) subbands; compute L1 between corresponding subbands, optionally up-weighting the three high-frequency subbands; add as loss = L1_pixel + w_wav*sum_subbands L1(subband). Can iterate one level or multi-level. Trivially cheap (a couple of fixed conv filters), fully differentiable, no extra networks.
- **Relevance:** A cheap, discriminator-free way to explicitly supervise the high-frequency subbands that carry AFHQ texture, complementary to LPIPS/DISTS and easy to add in a short run.

### Recent tokenizers

#### H3AE: High Compression, High Speed, and High Quality AutoEncoder for Video Diffusion Models (2025, arXiv preprint (Apr 2025); Snap Research)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.10567
- **What:** A systematic study of AE architecture, compression ratio, and training strategy for high-compression latent AEs, contributing a latent-consistency loss and an omni-training objective. Video-focused but the AE-training findings are image-transferable.
- **Why:** The strongest recent "training strategy" ablation adjacent to our lane: it explicitly compares training recipes and shows a simple Latent Consistency loss beating LPIPS/GAN/DWT auxiliaries in both quality and simplicity. For short training windows and a consumer GPU, that simplicity-with-gains result is directly actionable and lower-risk than adding a GAN stage. Complements DC-AE by informing what objective to use during the low-res pretrain and adaptation phases.
- **How:** Latent-consistency loss: encode an input, decode, re-encode the reconstruction, and penalize the distance between the two latents -- a self-consistency regularizer on the encoder/decoder that stabilizes and sharpens reconstruction without a discriminator. Combined with an omni-training objective to unify plain-AE and conditioned-AE. In our setting, drop the latent-consistency term into the DC-AE reconstruction loss as a cheap, GAN-free rFID booster during progressive/adaptation training.
- **Relevance:** Offers a simple, GAN-free latent-consistency loss shown to beat LPIPS/GAN for reconstruction quality -- a low-risk addition to DC-AE's phases well-suited to short AFHQ 128px training runs.

#### Masked Autoencoders Are Effective Tokenizers for Diffusion Models (MAETok) (2025, ICML 2025)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.03444
- **What:** A plain (non-variational) ViT autoencoder tokenizer trained with masked image modeling plus auxiliary feature-prediction heads, showing that a discriminative, well-structured latent space alone (no KL) yields SOTA reconstruction and generation with only 128 latent tokens.
- **Why:** Directly targets rFID: it hits 0.48 rFID on ImageNet 256 with a small ViT-Base encoder/decoder (176M total), and demonstrates the KL/variational term is unnecessary for fidelity. The mask-modeling + multi-target-feature recipe is a concrete, drop-in way to sharpen a small AE's latent and reconstructions in short training windows.
- **How:** ViT-Base encoder + ViT-Base decoder, 128 learnable latent tokens (hidden dim 32), 1D pos-emb on latents, 2D RoPE on patches. During training, mask 40-60% of image patch tokens with learnable mask tokens fed to the encoder. Three shallow (3-layer) auxiliary decoders predict HOG, DINOv2-L, and SigLIP-L features on the masked tokens via MSE (L_mask). Main loss L = L_recon + 1.0*L_percep(LPIPS) + 0.4*L_adv(GAN) + L_mask. Final stage: freeze encoder, fine-tune only the pixel decoder for 50K iters while linearly annealing mask ratio 60%->0%. No variational/KL loss.
- **Relevance:** Most transferable recipe for our small DC-AE on AFHQ: mask-modeling + a DINOv2/HOG auxiliary head plus decoder-only fine-tuning is a cheap way to cut rFID under a tight compute budget.

#### LiteVAE: Lightweight and Efficient Variational Autoencoders for Latent Diffusion Models (2024, NeurIPS 2024)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.14477
- **What:** A lightweight VAE that feeds a 2D discrete wavelet transform (DWT) of the image into a small feature-extraction/aggregation encoder, cutting encoder params ~6x while matching or beating SD-VAE on rFID/LPIPS/PSNR/SSIM.
- **Why:** The closest match to our constraint (small model, consumer GPU, short training): it shows how to reach SD-VAE-level reconstruction with a fraction of the encoder cost, and its decoder/training enhancements are concrete reconstruction-quality wins rather than pure efficiency tricks.
- **How:** Encode from multi-level 2D DWT coefficients (not raw pixels): a lightweight per-subband feature-extraction module (small UNet/conv nets) produces multiscale features, then a feature-aggregation module fuses them into one latent code. Decoder is a standard conv decoder with proposed enhancements plus training-dynamics improvements (loss weighting, adversarial/perceptual terms). Base model matches SD-VAE quality with 6x fewer encoder params; larger variant beats comparable VAEs on all four metrics.
- **Relevance:** A wavelet front-end plus a deliberately tiny encoder is a directly implementable way to get high-fidelity reconstruction on AFHQ 128px without the compute of a full conv VAE.


## Sweep 2 -- 2026-07-01 -- Breaking the rFID<2 barrier (8-lane deep sweep)

_Sweep 1 established the L1+VGG-LPIPS + capacity + stabilized-GAN recipe that took rFID 15.15 -> 6.54. Sweep 2 asks the harder question: what SPECIFICALLY breaks the <2 barrier (a further ~3.3x cut), given the fixed 16ch@16x16 (E=4096) latent interface, AFHQ's ~15k narrow images, and a single 16GB card. The load-bearing conclusion across all 8 lanes: the barrier is NOT latent capacity (we sit at 2x the E that ViTok/DC-AE need for rFID<2) and NOT conv-vs-ViT — it is a decoder + adversarial + small-data-training-regime problem, plus a measurement-honesty problem. Two families dominate the expected gain: (A) a properly-run modern GAN decoder-refinement, and (B) a generative (diffusion/flow or residual-diffusion) decoder that escapes the mean-seeking blur floor. Foundation-model feature distillation is the best non-interface "third lever". REPA/VA-VAE-style latent alignment is a generation lever and is explicitly OUT for rFID._

### Theme A -- The scaling-law reality check: E=4096 is NOT the bottleneck (measure it, then stop chasing it)

#### Learnings from Scaling Visual Tokenizers (ViTok) (2025, arXiv:2501.09755)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.09755
- **What:** Establishes that reconstruction rFID is log-linear in E = L*c (total latent floats) and puts E=4096 at rFID~1.6 pre-GAN / ~0.5 with GAN on ImageNet-256; decoder capacity is the dominant architectural lever after E (Base->Large decoder buys only ~0.3 rFID at fixed E), encoder size ~uncorrelated. EMA 0.9999 in the GAN stage.
- **Why (for <2):** This is the single most important framing result of the sweep. We are at E=4096 with rFID 6.54 — 3x+ worse than the paper at HALF the E they need for <2. Therefore adding latent channels almost certainly will NOT break the barrier; the gap is entirely training regime (GAN done right + enough steps + EMA) and decoder quality. It redirects effort away from the fixed-interface question, which is "free" (costs ~0 rFID to hold at 16ch).
- **How (in repo):** Cheaply PROVE it: sweep `--latent-dim {8,16,32,64}` at fixed 16x16 on the champion recipe, log val_rfid. Prediction: rFID roughly flat 16->32->64 (saturated). If it drops sharply, AFHQ is unexpectedly capacity-limited and an interface change is justified. Either way it removes ambiguity in ~3 short runs. Adopt EMA 0.9999 in phase-3 (Sweep 1 flagged this as planned-but-never-run).
- **Caveat:** All numbers are ImageNet-256; AFHQ is 100x smaller — the AFHQ E-vs-rFID curve must be measured, not assumed.

#### Deep Compression Autoencoder (DC-AE) + DC-AE 1.5 (2024/2025, arXiv:2410.10733 / 2508.00413)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.10733 , https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.00413
- **What:** DC-AE reaches rFID 0.69 at f32c32 (E=2048) on ImageNet with a residual CONV AE — our exact model family — proving conv is not the wrong architecture and 4096 floats is not the ceiling; the barrier is data/budget/GAN. DC-AE 1.5 adds a Structured/nested latent (ordered channel masking) that is rFID-neutral at full-c but gives a graceful multi-rate latent for downstream generation.
- **Why (for <2):** Confirms a conv AE at our float budget already reaches deep-sub-1 rFID *when trained at scale with a GAN* — so the sub-2 jump is a training/GAN/decoder problem, not an arch swap. The nested-latent is not an rFID lever but de-risks the fixed interface (lets the multistage generator use a truncated prefix).
- **How (in repo):** Optional nested-latent: after encode, sample c'~U{1..16}, decode from z*mask, apply L1+VGG-LPIPS on the masked decode (~15 lines, no model change, `--nested-latent`). Evaluate rFID at full-16 and at prefix-8 to characterize rate-distortion.
- **Caveat:** DC-AE's numbers require ImageNet-scale training with GAN; the structured-latent gain is measured on generation FID, not rFID.

### Theme B -- Modern adversarial training done RIGHT (the phase-3 lever that actually crosses <2)

#### The GAN is dead; long live the GAN! (R3GAN) (2024, NeurIPS 2024, arXiv:2501.05441)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.05441
- **What:** A minimal SOTA GAN baseline = relativistic pairing loss + BOTH zero-centered gradient penalties (R1 on real, R2 on fake), with a local-convergence proof that hinge/R1-only lack. R2 specifically prevents the fake-side gradient explosion that forces people to keep GAN weight tiny.
- **Why (for <2):** The GAN phase is the single biggest remaining lever — tokenizer ablations (ViTok 1.6->0.5, DC-AE 3.82->0.69) put ~68% of the rFID reduction on the adversarial stage. Our current phase-3 (spectral-norm PatchGAN + hinge + taming-lambda + fixed DiffAugment, ~180s, decoder-head-only) is a solid 2021-era recipe but under-regularized (hinge lacks the convergence guarantee, no R2) and under-trained. R3GAN's relativistic+R1+R2 is exactly the stability that lets the GAN run long on 15k images without collapse.
- **How (in repo):** In `dcae.py` replace `d_hinge_loss`/`g_hinge_loss` with the relativistic softplus pair; add R1 = grad of D(real) w.r.t. real inputs and R2 = grad of D(fake) w.r.t. fake inputs via `torch.autograd.grad(..., create_graph=True)`, penalty `(gamma/2)*(R1+R2)`, gamma~1-5 for 128px. Phase-3 already runs eager (good for double-backward). With R1+R2 stable you can likely raise `--gan-weight` from 0.5 toward 1.0.
- **Caveat:** R3GAN's numbers are generation FID on full datasets; the transferable mechanism is stable long GAN training on small data, which is exactly our bottleneck.

#### StyleGAN2-ADA (2020, NeurIPS, arXiv:2006.06676) + LeCam (2021, CVPR, arXiv:2104.03310)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.06676 , https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.03310
- **What:** ADA tracks the overfitting heuristic rt = E[sign(D(real))] and auto-tunes augmentation probability p to hold rt~0.6, the canonical fix for GANs on few-thousand-image sets (validated on AFHQ subclasses). LeCam anchors D(real)/D(fake) to EMAs — an f-divergence robust under limited data, complementary to R1/R2.
- **Why (for <2):** Our fixed-strength DiffAugment is a static approximation of ADA; on 15k images D overfits within hundreds of steps. Adaptive-p auto-throttles at the overfit boundary and is what lets the GAN phase run to full budget without the memorization that made naive GAN regress in v5. This is the multiplier that turns "GAN saturates at ~4" into "GAN reaches ~2.5".
- **How (in repo):** Generalize `diff_augment` into `diff_augment(x, p)` applying each transform with prob p; maintain an EMA of `disc(real).sign().mean()` as rt; after each disc step nudge `p += lr_p*sign(rt-0.6)`, clamp [0,0.85]. LeCam: track EMA anchors alpha_R/alpha_F, add `lecam_w*((real_logits-alpha_F)^2 + (fake_logits-alpha_R)^2)` to d_loss, weight ~0.001-0.01.
- **Caveat:** ADA's numbers are generation on AFHQ subclasses; reconstruction is an easier regime, so treat as a stabilizer/enabler, not a standalone rFID delta.

#### Generator EMA + give the GAN phase real capacity/budget (ViTok / VFM-VAE, arXiv:2501.09755 / 2510.18457)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.18457
- **What:** Every strong tokenizer evaluates FID on an EMA generator (0.999 for a short phase) and GAN-finetunes the WHOLE decoder at TARGET resolution for a substantial compute share — not one decoder block at 64px for 180s as our phase-3 does.
- **Why (for <2):** EMA is the most consistently-reported "free" FID drop (~0.3-1.0 rFID) and is currently MISSING (we eval the live oscillating decoder). Unfreezing the full decoder (encoder + to_latent frozen so the 16ch@16x16 interface is preserved) at 128px, longer, is what converts a *stable* GAN into a large rFID drop — you cannot buy sub-2 sharpness through a single decoder block.
- **How (in repo):** Snapshot EMA params of the trainable set, `ema.mul_(0.999).add_(param, alpha=0.001)` each step, eval/save from EMA. Change `decoder_head_prefixes` -> full-decoder prefixes ('decoder','head', optionally 'from_latent'; keep encoder/to_latent frozen). Set `--p3-res 128`, rebalance budgets toward p3. Watch 16GB: 128px + double-backward + full decoder may need batch 16 + grad-checkpointing.
- **Caveat:** Unfreezing from_latent mildly shifts latent stats; to_latent/encoder stay frozen so the downstream code is unchanged.

### Theme C -- Generative decoders: escaping the mean-seeking blur floor (the highest ceiling)

#### SSDD: Single-Step Diffusion Decoder + epsilon-VAE + DiTo (2025, arXiv:2510.04961 / 2410.04081 / 2501.18593)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.04961 , https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.04081 , https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.18593
- **What:** SSDD keeps the SAME KL-VAE encoder/latent and swaps only the deterministic decoder for a distilled single-step diffusion decoder: rFID 0.87->0.39 (-55%) at f8c4 on ImageNet, GAN-FREE, ~1x inference cost after distillation. epsilon-VAE's lite ablation: f16c8 rFID 11.15->6.24 (-44%) with a small 3-NFE decoder. DiTo shows diffusion autoencoders are scalable GAN-free tokenizers.
- **Why (for <2):** This is the strongest evidence-backed >2x lever at OUR compression, and it is INTERFACE-PRESERVING (same conv encoder, same 16ch@16x16 latent). L1+LPIPS+GAN on a deterministic decoder fundamentally regresses toward a blurred conditional mean; the last ~1-2 rFID of high-freq fur/whisker realism is easier to SAMPLE than to regress. A 40-55% cut on 6.54 lands ~3-3.9 alone; stacking a short GAN/distillation polish plausibly closes to <2. Bonus: it sidesteps the fragile GAN entirely.
- **How (in repo):** New `DiffusionDecoder` (small UNet/DiT-S, ~30-60M, latent injected via from_latent + nearest-upsample concat + adaLN on t). New `projects/multistage/diffdec.py`: freeze encoder from best.pt, train decoder with flow-matching (v-pred, logit-normal t) + 0.1*VGG-LPIPS on the x0 prediction; then distill 8-step teacher -> 1-2 step student. Eval = fixed-seed few-step ODE sample (deterministic) into the existing FID path. Keep bf16+channels_last; eager sampler.
- **Caveat:** All headline rFIDs are ImageNet-scale + long training; AFHQ's narrowness should help fidelity but its 1500-image eval makes FID noisier and the decoder must not overfit (use EMA + light aug).

#### HART residual-diffusion decoder head (2024, arXiv:2410.10812)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.10812
- **What:** A ~37M residual diffusion module bolted onto an existing tokenizer improved rFID 2.11->0.30 on MJHQ by generating only the high-freq residual the base decoder misses.
- **Why (for <2):** The lower-risk way to buy the generative-decoder win: keep the 6.54 conv decoder as a fast coarse decoder (a guaranteed floor — residual can be ~0), train a small refiner to predict (target - coarse) conditioned on the coarse recon + latent. The residual is low-energy high-freq (exactly AFHQ fur), so a tiny few-step refiner suffices. Strictly bounded below by 6.54.
- **How (in repo):** Freeze ConvAutoEncoder (best.pt); add `ResidualRefiner` (small UNet) conditioned on cat[coarse_recon, upsampled latent]; new phase, flow-matching on residual + VGG-LPIPS on (coarse+residual). Eval = coarse + fixed-seed 2-4 step residual sample. Interface untouched.
- **Caveat:** Residual-diffusion training is finicky; cited numbers are bigger-data/higher-res, so magnitude is extrapolated.

### Theme D -- Foundation-model feature distillation (the best non-interface "third lever")

#### MAETok + GigaTok (2025, ICML 2025 / ICCV 2025, arXiv:2502.03444 / 2504.08736)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.03444 , https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.08736
- **What:** MAETok reaches 0.48 rFID @128 tokens with a SMALL AE largely via shallow auxiliary heads predicting frozen DINOv2/HOG/SigLIP features. GigaTok is the cleanest evidence that semantic distillation IMPROVES rFID (not just gFID): cosine-align one DECODER layer to frozen DINOv2 -> rFID 0.94->0.89->0.81, resolving the reconstruction-vs-generation dilemma.
- **Why (for <2):** The best-evidenced non-interface lever after GAN — a frozen semantic prior sharpens a small decoder. On narrow AFHQ (in-distribution for DINOv2) the prior is especially strong, and 15k images can't teach good features from pixels alone, so the distillation is also a regularizer against overfit. Crucially it targets the DECODER rendering pathway (which diagnostics fingered as the rFID bottleneck), not the tiny latent. Composes with the GAN and the diffusion-decoder.
- **How (in repo):** Load frozen DINOv2-S (HF_HOME already set); add a small conv/MLP projector from a decoder mid-stage (32/64px) feature map to DINOv2 patch features; loss += `w_feat*(1-cos(proj, dino))`, w~0.05-0.5, heads dropped at inference. Add during phase1/2. Optionally add cheap HOG target. Watch throughput (DINO forward ~1.5-2x step cost; subsample or run at 64px).
- **Caveat:** Published magnitudes are ViT + ImageNet + long training; on a small conv decoder over 15k AFHQ the signal is weaker and may saturate — treat as a booster (~0.5-3 rFID plausible), not a <2 guarantee. NOTE: REPA/VA-VAE/U-REPA latent-to-foundation alignment is explicitly a GENERATION lever (rFID neutral-to-worse: VA-VAE 0.26->0.28) and belongs to the downstream generator lane, NOT here.

### Theme E -- Self-consistency & frequency: cheap on-recipe complements (minor levers)

#### EQ-VAE + Diffusability + H3AE (2025, arXiv:2502.09509 / 2502.14831 / 2504.10567)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.09509 , https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.14831 , https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.10567
- **What:** EQ-VAE enforces Encode(T(x))~=T(Encode(x)) for small rot/scale transforms — the ONE self-contained result that lowers rFID when fine-tuned onto an existing AE (VQGAN 7.94->7.54, SD-VAE 0.90->0.82, never regresses). H3AE's re-encode latent-consistency (encode->decode->re-encode, penalize latent drift) was the one aux loss that helped in their ablation (+0.1 PSNR). Diffusability makes latents scale-equivariant so a diffusion decoder inverts them more easily.
- **Why (for <2):** Not standalone barrier-breakers (~5-10% rFID at best) but cheap, low-risk, self-contained polish that stacks on the GAN and — critically — makes the latent smoother/diffusable, which is a direct MULTIPLIER on the Theme-C diffusion-decoder path (cleaner latent -> smaller/faster decoder reaches the same rFID). EQ-VAE is the highest-EV cheap bet: it cannot cost the fixed interface and empirically never hurts rFID.
- **How (in repo):** EQ-VAE as an optional fine-tune phase: sample T from {rot90, flip, interp-scale}, apply to z, decode, add L1+VGG-LPIPS vs same-T-transformed image, `--eq-reg`. H3AE: `z2=encode(rec).detach(); loss += w*(z-z2).abs().mean()`, `--latent-consistency`. Both ~15-20 lines, pure tensor ops, bf16/compile-friendly.
- **Caveat:** EQ-VAE/H3AE gains are ImageNet/video and on more-converged AEs; AFHQ magnitude is smaller and speculative — the safe claim is "doesn't cost reconstruction, smooths the latent for downstream + diffusion decode".

#### DISTS + wavelet-subband L1 / FA-VAE dual-path head (2020/2025, arXiv:2004.07728 / 2509.05441 / 2503.14325)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.07728 , https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.05441
- **What:** DISTS adds explicit texture statistics with deformation tolerance LPIPS lacks (SoftVQ-VAE ablation: LPIPS + a second complementary perceptual loss beats either alone). Wavelet-subband L1 on LH/HL/HH directly supervises the high-freq band L1+LPIPS structurally under-fit. FA-VAE's real gains (~65% recon / ~80% HF error cut) came from an ARCHITECTURAL frequency-decoupled dual-path decoder, NOT a loss term.
- **Why (for <2):** Pure loss swaps are MINOR (0.2-0.7 rFID) and partly redundant with the GAN (both attack the HF band). DISTS is the best cheap perceptual add (torchmetrics-native, texture-focused, complementary to VGG-LPIPS — good fit for AFHQ fur). The one loss-lane idea with a >1-point ceiling is FA-VAE's architectural dual-path head (a lightweight HF branch predicting Haar subbands, combined via inverse DWT) — interface-preserving (both paths decode the same latent), but high effort.
- **How (in repo):** DISTS: torchmetrics `DeepImageStructureAndTextureSimilarity`, add `w_dists*dists` (w~0.25-0.5) alongside LPIPS. Wavelet: fixed 4-tap Haar depthwise conv, L1 on the 3 HF subbands, `--wave-weight` 0.05-0.2, apply in phase1/2. Dual-path head: parallel decoder branch outputting 9ch HF subbands + inverse Haar DWT, supervised by HF-L1 + PatchGAN, `--wavelet-head`. Deprioritize FFL (redundant global-spectrum penalty; keep at weight 0 as an ablation baseline — wavelet-subband L1 strictly dominates it).
- **Caveat:** FA-VAE's 65% is ImageNet-16x with full dual encoder+decoder; a decoder-head-only lite version captures a fraction. Do NOT promise the 65%.

### Theme F -- Measurement honesty & training-regime reality (do these FIRST, near-free)

#### Rethinking FID + longer-training reality (2024/2025, arXiv:2401.09603 / 2501.01423)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.09603
- **What:** FID on <10-20k images is unreliable and biased UPWARD; the literature wants >=10-50k. Separately, every sub-2 tokenizer trains 50-125 epochs / ~100k steps with EMA — our champion got ~14 epochs (~6-7k steps), 10-20x too short, and the "longer training buys nothing" conclusion is an artifact of fitting an exponential to <=16-epoch runs under a fixed 5-min budget (the documented time-budget x cosine confound).
- **Why (for <2):** Two cheap reality anchors before spending GPU on new architectures. (1) An unknown chunk of 6.54 is small-sample noise on 1500 eval images — re-measuring on ~10-15k recon pairs may show we are already closer to <2 than 6.54 suggests, and it tells us the TRUE distance. (2) A single long direct-128px run (drop the 5-min cap, ~30-60k steps, EMA on, one long cosine) plausibly reaches the ~3-5 pre-GAN floor the schedule never actually reached — the load-bearing prerequisite for the GAN to finish <2. On AFHQ (100x smaller/narrower than ImageNet) the ImageNet floors are conservative.
- **How (in repo):** Eval: build real+recon feature banks from ~10-15k pairs (reuse train-split recons or deterministic flips), report rFID at 1.5k vs 15k with a confidence band. Training: `--time-budget 3600` (or `--epochs 60`) at 128px, bc64, L1, VGG 0.1, EMA on, single cosine to ~5% floor, largest batch that fits; ~22min at 656 img/s.
- **Caveat:** The larger-sample rFID is a re-anchor, not a reducer; longer training's floor on AFHQ is a conservative lower bound, exact value must be measured.

#### DiffAugment (Sweep-1) + ImageNet-pretrain transfer (2020/2023, arXiv:2006.10738 / 2305.02541)
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.02541
- **What:** Adding more in-domain animal-face data (AFHQ-v2, iNaturalist mammal/bird crops) mainly stabilizes the GAN (stops D memorizing 15k images) rather than directly cutting rFID. The crazier variant: pretrain the tokenizer on ImageNet to inherit a near-sub-2 texture/decoder prior, then fine-tune the decoder + short GAN on AFHQ — FA-VAE shows ImageNet-trained AEs zero-shot transfer faithfully to faces.
- **Why (for <2):** Data is a real but SECONDARY lever here (AFHQ is already narrow and not data-starved per diagnostics); its value is enabling a longer/stronger GAN. ImageNet-pretrain is the highest-upside "borrow the floor" bet — the hard part (natural-image texture + decoder rendering) is solved at scale; AFHQ fine-tune only specializes it.
- **How (in repo):** Concat extra image folders into the train datamodule (keep AFHQ test split fixed). Pretrain: train ConvAutoEncoder on the existing ImageNet streaming datamodule (src/chimera/data/imagenet.py) at 16ch@16x16, then `--resume` on AFHQ.
- **Caveat:** ImageNet-pretrain is high integration effort; if warm-starting an off-the-shelf f8 VAE it is an interface change (its latent is 4ch or a different layout — call out the tradeoff; matching 16ch@16x16 preserves the multistage interface).
